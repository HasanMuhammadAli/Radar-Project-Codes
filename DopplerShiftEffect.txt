Understanding the Doppler Shift Effect
The Doppler effect is the change in frequency (or wavelength) of a wave due to the relative motion between the source and the observer.

Doppler Effect in Radar Systems
When an object moves towards a radar, the frequency of the received signal increases.

When an object moves away from a radar, the frequency of the received signal decreases.
